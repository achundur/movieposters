Literary Masochist

"You strike me as an appreciator of tragic problematic female protagonists"

How it came to be

Ever since I read The Poppy War Trilogy by R.F. Kuang and it left me emotionally distraught for 3 months, I became obsessed with finding books that would have me experience the full spectrum of human emotions. In 2022, I had the best reading year since I was 13. I think I read 56 books that year; I found so many incredible authors and books. Looking back at those 56 books I read, it became clear there was a pattern of books I was drawn too. Including but not limited to: dark, epic grimdark fantasy / sci-fi, mythology retellings, space operas, and historical fiction.


In discussions with my friends about books, it may have come up a few times.





From my own twitter

Design inspiration: Dark academia & library books

I felt that this would be the perfect opportunity for a dark academia concept. I loved the posters for Little Women and Saint Maud- the visuals of the hair and the atmospheric mood were nice.

The design process


I definitely struggled a bit with the typography and design for this poster. I took an eight month break between the last poster (Cheese Queen) and this one, so I was definitely rusty. The other challenge was figuring out how to include all of my favorite quotes (see above) while still making the poster look good. I did a lot of iterations (see below).



Here are some of the other versions I was considering




I was really torn between this one and the final. I really really wanted to find a way to include all of the quotes, but it ended up not working out. My main takeaway from this process: "kill your darlings."
